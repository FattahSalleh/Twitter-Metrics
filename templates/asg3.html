Top 10 Nodes by Betweeness Centrality

Betweeness Centrality tests how relevant a node is for the shortest network paths.

It is a measure of how important the node is for information flow over a network.

Even though MarkRuffalo has many social connections, AmandaImani91 is the common node for nodes having the shortest route to other nodes.

We can say AmandaImani91 and MarkRuffalo are in between few groups in the network graph.

Top 10 Nodes by Eigenvector Centrality

Eigenvector Centrality is a measure of a node's influence in a network.

A high eigenvector score means a node is connected to many nodes that have high scores themselves.

MarkRuffalo has the highest Eigenvector Centrality while the second node - ChrisEvans - falls quite far behind him.

Can assume that MarkRuffalo has many followers who are as famous as him.

Top 10 Nodes by Closeness Centrality

Closeness centrality is a way to detect nodes that can very easily spread information through a network.

Nodes with a high score of closeness have the shortest distances to any other node.

Watsonsmy has the highest score in the rankings.

Watsonmy is at the core of the network and this is similar to our research on followers and friends of friends of Watsons.

Top 10 Nodes by Clustering Coefficient

A clustering coefficient is a measure of the degree to which nodes appear to cluster together in one graph.

The clustering coefficient is high when these connections are dense.

If all of your "mates" know each other, then you have a high coefficient of clustering and vice versa.

From the calculations, the charts above show the top 10 with the same 1.0 score.

It can be assumed that most of the users are mutuals.

Average Clustering Coefficient = 0.07122

Even though the top clusters in this network has 1.0 score of clustering coefficient, however the average of clustering coefficient is still low.

This means that there are still many other clusters with low clustering coefficient.

Their followers don't know each other or not related at all.
